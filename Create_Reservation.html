Caribou Munroes Provincial Park

Just a few kilometres from one of Nova Scotia's points of entry, Caribou/Munroes Island Provincial Park makes a marvelous stop on your way to or from Prince Edward Island. A mile long sand beach offers some of the warmest saltwater swimming north of the Carolinas and the campground provides excellent opportunities for camping, or just appreciating nature. The park offers 95 sites, picnic areas, and an unsupervised beach. There is a walking trail along the beach out to the island. WiFi is available at the admin building and surrounding area. Password required; please see the park receptionist. Caribou/Munroes Island Provincial Park is located 11 km (7 mi.) north of the junctions of Routes 6, 106, and 376 on Nova Scotia's Northumberland Shore, 4 km (2.5 mi.) east of the Caribou / Wood Islands (PEI) ferry wharf.
